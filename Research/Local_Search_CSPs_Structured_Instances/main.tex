\subsection{Definitions and preliminaries}

\begin{definition}{Boolean Relation}{boolrel}
	For an integer $r \in \NN^+$, a \emph{boolean relation} $R$ is a subset of $\FF_2^r$, where the integer $r$ is called the \emph{arity} of $R$.
\end{definition}

For a boolean relation $R$ of arity $r$ and $b \in \FF_2^r$, define the boolean relation $R \;\oplus \;b \coloneqq \{\alpha \oplus b \ | \ \alpha \in R\}$, where $\oplus$ is the addition operator over $\FF^r_2$. When considering $R$ ias a clause in a CSP instance, $\oplus$ operator can be seen as variable negations. For a relation $R$ with index $[r]$ and $S \subseteq [r]$, define the projection relation $\pi_s(R) = \{\alpha \; | \; \alpha \in R\}$, where $\alpha_S$ means extracting the value on coordinates in $S$.
