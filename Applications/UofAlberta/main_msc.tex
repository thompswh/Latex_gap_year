{\LARGE Thesis Abstract}

\vspace*{2mm}
Whitaker Thompson 

\end{centering}

\noindent\rule{\textwidth}{0.4pt}

The Unique Games problem is central to the theory of hardness of approximation; it is conjectured to be $\NP$-hard to distinguish between almost and barely satisfiable instances in the general case. This conjecture implies the optimality of approximaton algorithms for several canonical $\NP$-hard problems, like \textsc{VertexCover} and \textsc{MaxCut}.

In this thesis, we study the Unique Games problem when the constraint graph is a metric graph, i.e.\ a complete graph equipped with a weight that obeys the triangle inequality. We give a randomized polynomial-time algorithm that selects a pivot and then rounds a solution to an extended linear-programming relaxation of the problem. Our algorithm is simple and achieves a 12-approximation in expectation, which separates our case from the general case, assuming the Unique Games Conjecture.
